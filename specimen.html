A constraint system for agents: cool graph-paper surfaces, mono-set data, hairline structure, one Signal Blue accent. Every rule on this page is also obeyed by this page.

01Invariants

Ten rules that make a page Ori

  1. Cool canvas — Frost #FAFBFE; white only on cards; warm tones never.
  2. One accent — Signal Blue #2F80ED. Navy is structure, not accent.
  3. Sans + mono only. Serif banned; italics banned — emphasis is weight or blue.
  4. Every meaningful number is mono with tabular figures.
  5. Hairlines are 1px; the only thick line is the 2px blue thread.
  6. The 6px node square appears at least once per page, at most once per component.
  7. Flush-left axis, ragged right. Centering only on slide covers and heros.
  8. Sharp geometry: radius 0 by default, 2px chips, 6px cards. No pills.
  9. Flat: no print shadows; screen gets lift and hover only; one sanctioned gradient (night hero).
  10. Color means data — green/red for deltas only; nothing decorated with color.

04Signature elements

Meta rail, thread rule, node — and one crisp mark

Three moves make a page recognizably Ori, and all three come from the brand mark's interlaced geometry: the meta rail you saw at the top of this page, the eyebrow + thread rule opening this section (a 2px thread anchored by the 6px node square), and the node footer that closes every printed page. Section indexes are two-digit mono because Ori documents argue in sequence.

The logo follows one rule per surface: the mark rides the meta rail at cap height on every branded page (white on night), covers add the wordmark as a bottom-left imprint, and resumes stay unbranded. The logo is always small, crisp, and complete — never a watermark, never cropped, never tinted, never redrawn.

Insight This callout is the fourth signature: a wash strip with a 2px blue thread, used once per section to carry the conclusion — the “so what”, never decoration.
